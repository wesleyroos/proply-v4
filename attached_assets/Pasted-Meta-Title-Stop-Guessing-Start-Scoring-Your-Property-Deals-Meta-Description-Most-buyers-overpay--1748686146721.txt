What Is a Deal Score?
How do we put an end to this expensive guesswork? The answer is a Deal Score – a smart, data-driven rating for any property you’re looking to buy. Think of a Deal Score like a credit score for your property deal: it crunches multiple factors and boils them down into an easy-to-understand grade or number. A smart, data-driven rating for any property
A Deal Score is not a random number – it’s powered by actual data and analytics. Behind that simple score is a mini army of property information: recent sales in the area, the home’s appraised value, potential rental income, neighborhood trends, and more. The tool looks at the property from an investor’s perspective, evaluating how good of a deal it is at the asking price. For example, if a house is listed at R2,000,000 but comparable sales in the suburb average R1.8m, and the rental yield is below the area average, the Deal Score will reflect that – likely coming in low to signal a poor deal. On the other hand, if you’ve found a gem priced under market value or in a high-growth area, expect a high Deal Score indicating a great deal. In essence, the Deal Score gives any buyer or investor instant insight that would normally require a seasoned appraiser or hours of research. It’s smart, automated, and works for any residential property in South Africa – whether it’s a studio flat in Cape Town or a family home in Durban.
What goes into the score (value, yield, area, traffic, etc.)
You might be wondering, how exactly is the Deal Score calculated? While the exact algorithm is proprietary, the score typically incorporates four key pillars of a property’s investment potential:
Value vs. Price: This looks at how the asking price compares to fair market value. It uses data from recent sales (comparative market analysis) to see if you’re overpaying or getting a bargain. If the property’s price is well above what data says it’s worth, the score drops. If it’s below or in line with market value, the score improves.


Rental Yield: Even if you’re buying to live in, rental yield is a great reality check on a property’s financial performance. The Deal Score factors in what rent the property could fetch versus its price. A higher yield (rental income as a percentage of price) means the home could pay for itself quicker – a positive sign, boosting the score. A low yield (say a pricey home with little rental demand) will drag the score down.


Area & Growth: Location, location, location – the Deal Score digs into area trends. Is the suburb growing in value year-on-year or stagnating? What’s the average price per square meter in that street? Are people moving in (high demand) or moving out? It may also consider factors like local amenities or upcoming infrastructure that could affect future value. A property in a high-growth, in-demand area will score higher than one in a declining or oversupplied location.


Demand & “Traffic”: This is about how much interest the property is generating. For instance, if a listing has been sitting for 100 days with few enquiries, that’s a red flag (maybe overpriced or issues). High “traffic” – like thousands of views on Property24 or multiple offers – can indicate strong demand (or occasionally, underpricing). Deal Score takes into account these signals of market interest and liquidity. A property no one else wants might score low (there could be a reason), whereas a hot property in a competitive market might score high if the fundamentals (value and yield) also check out.


All these components are weighed and combined into your final Deal Score, typically on a scale (for example, 0 to 100 or 1 to 10). A higher score = better deal. For example, a Deal Score of 85/100 might mean “excellent deal – likely undervalued and high potential”. A score of 50 might mean “average – fair price but nothing special”. A low score, say 20/100, screams “poor deal – overpriced or risky”. The beauty is you don’t have to be a data analyst to get it. One glance at the score and you have a solid, unbiased assessment of the property’s worthiness. It’s the death of guesswork, replaced by clear-eyed analysis.
